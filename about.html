Our Story

EcoPrint didn’t begin in a boardroom ,it was born on the streets and shores of Mombasa, Kenya, where plastic waste had become both a crisis and a calling. five young changemakers from Mombasa who came together during the Mombasa

Plastic Prize Competition, a challenge designed to inspire solutions to the plastic waste crisis. As we worked, we realized we shared something bigger than an idea: a deep frustration with how our communities were being left behind in both sustainability and education and a determination to do something about it.

We saw how students in under-resourced schools were struggling with STEM subjects because they had never touched a lab model, and how plastic waste was choking our environment. What if the solution to one problem could solve the other?

We visson a world where every piece of plastic tells a new story ,one of education, innovation, and a planet restored by the very youth it once endangered.

So we have a mission to reduce environmental pollution and transform access to quality STEM education by converting plastic waste into impactful 3D printing solutions for schools and communities.

Our Impact

Quality Education

We’ve equipped 10+ public schools with 3D-printed STEM models, giving 400+ students hands-on science learning they never had before.

Decent Work & Growth

EcoPrint creates green jobs, trains youth in 3D tech, and empowers waste workers through plastic buy-back and skill-building opportunities.

Responsible Consumption

We turn waste into useful products through a circular process from trash to 3D filament to classroom tools all with zero waste in production.

Climate Action

We have diverted over 17 tonnes of plastic waste from landfills and burning sites helping reduce emissions and protect the environment.With a production having zero carbon emission

Life Below Water

By recovering plastic from beaches and coastal communities, we are protecting marine ecosystems from plastic pollution before it reaches the ocean.

Life on Land

Our clean-up and recycling efforts reduce plastic waste in informal settlements and landfills helping restore cleaner, safer communities.
